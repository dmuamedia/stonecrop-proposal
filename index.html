04 Faith-Based Investing

We didn't build this for the RFP. We've run it since 2018.

In 2018, we formally created and began implementing Faith-Based model portfolios, starting with Doug MacGray's own personal portfolio, before proactively recommending it to clients once we had a five-year track record.

Part of Stonecrop's mission is to move more investment capital, especially that of Christians and Christian institutions, into companies committed to Christian principles. This lets clients like GCTS avoid profiting from certain activities, while helping a growing Faith-Based investing industry exercise positive influence over the corporations it invests in, and the culture at large.

Just because a fund includes "Catholic," "Faith," "Values," or "Impact" in its name doesn't mean it's authentically faith-based, in our view. Continued vigilance is required, and we exercise it on behalf of every client.

Our screening process
  1. 01

    Identify fund companies that claim to be Faith-Based.

  2. 02

    Review their positive & negative screening methodology for meaning and authenticity.

  3. 03

    Apply screening technologies, eVALUEator and Inspire, and review holdings via YCharts.

  4. 04

    At GCTS's scale, consider separately managed accounts, screened with greater precision.
